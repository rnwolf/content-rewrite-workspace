# **Fire safety of your building: for tenants and residents**

Published 30 August 2017

If you have any concerns regarding the safety of your building, you should:

* in the first instance, contact your landlord or building owner
* if you are still concerned, you should then contact your local authority or local fire and rescue service for advice
* if you do not feel that the issue has been sufficiently dealt with, you can [contact](https://forms.communities.gov.uk/) the Ministry of Housing, Communities and Local Government building safety team

[Search for your local council](https://www.gov.uk/find-local-council)
[Search for your local fire and rescue service](https://fireengland.uk/your-fire-and-rescue-service/find-your-service)

You may also find the following information and resources useful:

* High rise safety for residents: [National Fire Chiefs Council](https://www.nationalfirechiefs.org.uk/High--Rise-Safety-for-Residents)
* [Fire safety guidance inside and outside the home](https://www.gov.uk/government/collections/fire-safety-guidance#fire-safety-guidance-for-inside-and-outside-the-home)
* [Make your home safe from fire](https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/make-your-home-safe-from-fire)
* [Information on fire safety in shared or rented accommodation](https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/fire-safety-in-shared-or-rented-accommodation)
* [Information about repairs and maintenance of council properties](https://www.gov.uk/council-housing/repairs-and-maintenance)
* [Guidance about completing a fire safety risk assessment for people responsible for sleeping accommodation](https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/fire-safety-risk-assessment-sleeping-accommodation)

In an emergency, you should always dial 999
